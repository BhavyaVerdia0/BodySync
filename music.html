YOGA

Vairagya Chant

Nirvana Shatakam

Nirvana Shatakam, composed by Adi Shankara himself, embodies the spiritual pursuit

Nirvana means "formless" The Nirvana Shatakam is towards this – you don’t want to be either this or that. If you don’t want to be this nor that, then what do you want to be? Your mind cannot understand this because your mind always wants to be something. If I say, “I don’t want to be this; I don’t want to be that,” you would think, “Oh something super!” Not super. “Oh, so emptiness?” Not emptiness. “Nothingness?” Not nothingness. That’s what is being conveyed through this chant.

Mantra

Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra

Meaning:We worship the three-eyed One, who is fragrant and who nourishes all. Like the fruit falls off from the bondage of the stem, may we be liberated from death, from mortality.

Bhajan

Ramayan Chaupai

It is a popular devotional bhajan that narrates the divine story of Lord Rama, as told in the Ramayana. The lyrics of this bhajan express devotion and reverence towards Lord Rama and depict the joyous singing and history telling of his divine life. Through melodious tunes and heartfelt lyrics,It invites devotees to immerse themselves in the timeless tale of Lord Rama's virtuous life, teachings, and ultimate triumph of good over evil. It serves as a means to connect with the divine and find solace, inspiration, and spiritual upliftment in the sacred text of the Ramayana.
