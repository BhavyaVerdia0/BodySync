BREAKFAST

Porridge

INGREDIENTS
1/2 cup of rolled oats or quinoa flakes 1 cup of water or your choice of milk (almond, coconut, cow's milk, etc.) 1 tablespoon of ghee (clarified butter) or coconut oil 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon of ground cardamom 1/4 teaspoon of ground ginger 1 tablespoon of chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts, or cashews) 1 tablespoon of raisins or chopped dates 1 tablespoon of honey or maple syrup (optional)
INSTRUCTIONS
Boil oats or quinoa flakes in water/milk. Stir in cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, and ghee. Add chopped nuts and dried fruits. Optionally sweeten with honey or maple syrup. Remove from heat and serve hot. Enjoy mindfully for a nourishing start.
400 CAL, 11 G PROTEIN, 11 G FAT
Suitable for all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha)

MORNING SNACKS

Roasted Chickpeas

INGREDIENTS
1/4 cup cooked chickpeas (drained and rinsed) 1/2 teaspoon ghee or coconut oil Pinch of ground cumin Pinch of ground coriander Pinch of ground turmeric Pinch of Himalayan salt
INSTRUCTIONS
Roast chickpeas with a drizzle of ghee or coconut oil. Sprinkle with Ayurvedic spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and a pinch of Himalayan salt. Bake until crunchy and enjoy as a savory snack. Now serve it and enjoy the delicious snack. 80 CAL, 7.5 G PROTEIN, 2.5 G FAT
Suitable for all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha)

LUNCH

Lentil Dal with Steamed Vegetables

INGREDIENTS
1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed 3 cups water 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon mustard seeds 1 onion, finely chopped 2 garlic cloves, minced 1-inch piece of ginger, grated 1 tomato, chopped 1 teaspoon ground turmeric 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin Salt, to taste Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped for garnish Lemon wedges, for serving
For Steamed Vegetables: Assorted vegetables such as carrots, broccoli, cauliflower, and green beans Water for steaming
INSTRUCTIONS
Cook Lentils: Boil 3 cups of water in a pot. Add rinsed red lentils and cook until soft, about 20-25 minutes. Set aside. Prepare Vegetables: Chop assorted vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Steam until tender, about 10-15 minutes. Tempering: Heat ghee or coconut oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Saut√© chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger until translucent. Add Spices: Incorporate chopped tomato, ground turmeric, ground coriander, and ground cumin. Cook until tomatoes break down and the mixture becomes aromatic. Combine and Season: Mix cooked lentils with tempered spices. Adjust consistency with water if needed. Season with salt. Simmer briefly to meld flavors. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with cilantro leaves. Serve hot with steamed vegetables and lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy your Ayurvedic Lentil Dal with Steamed Vegetables, a nourishing and balanced meal! 300 CAL, PROTEIN 15 G, FAT 8 G
Suitable for all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha)

EVENING SNACKS

Fruit Chaat

INGREDIENTS
1 apple, diced 1 pear, diced 1 cup grapes, halved 1 orange, segmented 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala (a blend of spices including cumin, coriander, amchur, etc.) 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice 1/4 teaspoon black salt (kala namak), optional Fresh mint leaves, chopped, for garnish
INSTRUCTIONS
Dice an apple and a pear, halve grapes, and segment an orange. Mix the fruits in a plate Sprinkle with chaat masala and squeeze fresh lemon juice over the fruits. Optionally sprinkle with black salt and garnish with chopped mint leaves. Serve immediately and enjoy!
Protein: 1-2 grams, Fat: Negligible (less than 0.5 grams), Sugar: 10-15 grams
Suitable for all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha)

Protein: 8-10 grams, Fat:5-7 grams, Calories: 200-250 calories
